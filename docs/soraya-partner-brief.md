# Soraya / Kaleidoworks
## Partner Brief — May 2026

---

### The Problem in One Sentence

AI is arriving faster than the human judgment required to use it well — and the institutions responsible for building that judgment are already struggling.

---

### What Is Happening

Three signals are converging at the same moment.

**Learning disruption.** The National Assessment of Educational Progress reported in 2022 that average math scores for nine-year-olds fell seven points compared to 2020 — the first decline of its kind in the history of that long-term trend series. Reading dropped five points. These declines cut across racial and ethnic groups. Many students entering the workforce over the next decade will have thinner foundational skills in the areas most critical for AI-age work: reading with comprehension, evaluating claims, and sequencing complex tasks.

**Workforce skill pressure.** The World Economic Forum's Future of Jobs Report 2025 identifies analytical thinking as the top core skill, with seven out of ten companies considering it essential, followed by resilience, flexibility, agility, and leadership. These are precisely the executive-function skills that AI use tends to erode when the tool becomes a replacement rather than an extension of human judgment.

**Enterprise AI risk.** Nearly half of U.S. employees used AI tools at least occasionally by late 2025. Deloitte reports that risk management and regulatory compliance are the top concerns for organizations scaling generative AI. McKinsey finds that roughly half of employees worry about AI inaccuracy. NIST has defined the core failure mode — generative AI confabulation — as confidently presented false content that migrates into workflow and decision-making without adequate human review.

These are not three separate problems. They share a root cause: the human executive functions required to use AI responsibly — planning, prioritization, verification, follow-through, and judgment under uncertainty — are not being built. They are being bypassed.

---

### The Market Thesis

The scarce resource in the AI age will not be access to powerful models. It will be disciplined human agency: the ability to prioritize, verify, make accountable decisions, and follow through.

The competitive advantage for individuals, teams, and institutions will not come from having better AI. It will come from having better human-AI operating discipline — people who can direct AI well, catch what it gets wrong, and take responsibility for the outcome.

No existing product category is built around this thesis. General AI assistants optimize for engagement and task completion. Learning management systems manage compliance, not capability development. HR platforms track training hours, not agency growth. The gap is real and widening.

We call the category **Executive Function Assurance**: systems that help people and organizations preserve human agency, verification, prioritization, and follow-through while using AI.

---

### The Product: Soraya

Soraya is a human-centered AI coaching architecture designed to strengthen executive function without replacing it. It detects cognitive-state signals — ambiguity, overload, avoidance, time blindness, emotional drag, dependency risk — and responds with bounded, agency-preserving interventions. It helps users move from overwhelm to orientation, from vague intent to concrete next action, and from AI dependence to practiced human capability.

Soraya is not a general-purpose chatbot. It is not a therapist, clinician, or compliance system. It is a governed coaching layer that sits alongside AI tooling and asks a different question than most AI products: *Is the human getting more capable, or less?*

The architecture has three distinguishing features.

**Cognitive-state detection and adaptive modes.** Soraya reads executive-function signals in real time and maps them to specific intervention modes — Clarify, Triage, Tiny Step, Reset, Momentum, Reflection, Safety. Each mode is designed to reduce unnecessary friction while returning meaningful choice and action ownership to the user.

**The Agency Ledger.** Soraya tracks whether user-executed agency is increasing or decreasing over time. If the system is performing more of the meaningful cognition while the user performs less, dependency risk rises and the system shrinks its footprint. This is the inverse of engagement maximization — and it is the core behavioral differentiator.

**Governance spine compatibility.** Soraya is designed as a domain pack within a broader AI governance model (C3-ICM / LRAI). Governance controls permission and risk escalation. Soraya controls support style and cognitive scaffolding. Safety routing always dominates coaching optimization.

### Go-to-Market

Soraya enters through four focused pilot verticals rather than a broad consumer launch.

**Soraya Learn** — for students, tutors, adult learners, and training programs. Primary outcome: task initiation, study planning, comprehension verification, and follow-through without answer outsourcing.

**Soraya Work** — for professionals and teams. Primary outcome: prioritization, overwhelm reduction, meeting preparation, and execution checkpoints.

**Soraya Onboard** — for HR, training departments, and managers. Primary outcome: new-hire role clarity, workflow orientation, and responsible AI-use habits from day one.

**Soraya Govern** — for AI governance, compliance, and operations teams. Primary outcome: verification discipline, route-aware escalation, and audit-friendly human-agency logs.

The strongest early institutional buyers are mid-to-large employers concerned about AI overreliance, higher education institutions responding to academic integrity pressure, and workforce development programs building AI-age employability skills.

The go-to-market language is simple and stakeholder-safe: *Soraya builds executive-function capacity for responsible AI use. It helps people start, structure, verify, and finish. It helps organizations reduce AI-driven noise and preserve accountable human judgment.*
